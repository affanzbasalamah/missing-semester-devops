## Why this exists

Universities teach algorithms and theory. Jobs demand people who can stand up a server,
debug a network, harden a system, and automate all of it. Almost nobody teaches the gap
in between — how computers, networks, and production systems *actually* work.

Inspired by [MIT's Missing Semester](https://missing.csail.mit.edu/), this curriculum
closes that gap for DevOps and cybersecurity — but with a twist: **everything is built on
real, cheap hardware you own**. A used micro PC becomes your server. An OpenWrt router
becomes your network. Your own homelab hosts your portfolio.

## The closed loop

This curriculum has a signature: **your documentation site is both the deliverable and the proof.**
You self-host your own blog and git repositories on infrastructure you built, reachable through
an overlay network you configured, secured by hardening you applied. When an employer visits
your portfolio, the fact that the page loads at all *is* your résumé.

## How to use this site

- **Self-paced.** Every module has standalone notes, labs, and a checkpoint checklist. No lectures required.
- **Hardware-first.** Check the [hardware guide](/guides/hardware/) — the full lab costs less than one certification exam.
- **Public by default.** From Module 0 onward, everything you do lives in a public git repo. Your progress *is* your portfolio.
- **Finished?** Submit your self-hosted site to the [showcase](/guides/showcase/).
